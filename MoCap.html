Kinematic and dynamic validation of OpenCap motion capture systemthrough Xsens IMUs

Semester Project – Sport Biomechanics and evaluation of human performances

Since human motion analysis has become more and more used in fields that are not directly related to the clinical one, it is necessary to find more intuitive and user-friendly acquisition systems as OpenCap.The main objective of this work was to validate the OpenCap motion capture system comparing the acquisitions’ results with the Xsens’ ones, a leading provider of inertial sensors.

The validation was conducted both from a kinematic and a dynamic point of view. The kinematic analysis, performed through the use of MAE and ROM, led to a positive correlation between the systems’ acquisitions in terms of knee and ankle flexion angles, while hip angles showed insufficient results. From a dynamic point of view, after the computation of GRF profiles, the analysis of jump height’s MAE showed comparable results between OpenCap and Xsens systems.

Furthermore, an evaluation of the relationship between height of the jump and different parameters had been conducted, bringing to the conclusion that Peak Power was the most affective one.
